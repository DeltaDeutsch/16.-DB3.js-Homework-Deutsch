Deutsch Data Journalism and D3

The data set included with the assignment is based on 2014 estimates from the US Census Bureau, but is relevant for indicating possibly strong relationships between location and health: income, obesity, poverty, etc. by state. MOE stands for "margin of error."

D3 Dabbler Insight

This scatter plot shows possible strong relationships between the following pairs of data variables (X-Axis variables: In Poverty (%), Lacks Healthcare (%), Median Income ($) Y-Axis variables: Smokes (%), Obesity (%), Median Age). This is an INTERACTIVE chart. Hit the X or Y axis indicators to switch views of the different relationships between variables. The indicators CLOSEST to the X or Y axis are the ones being analyzed. Mouse over the circles for each state to obtain specific information on that state.

A Few, Quick Observations

  • West Virginia and Kentucky indicate more poverty results in more people smoking
  • Colorado has the lowest obesity nationwide.
  • Mississipi, West Virginia, and Arkansas have both the highest obesity and lowest median income.
  • New Hamshire is the state with the lowest % of the population in poverty
  • Texas has the most people as a % of total population lacking healthcare
